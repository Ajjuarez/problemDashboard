HPI: This 71-year-old white female presents for follow up of congestive heart failure and uncontrolled hypertension. Patient continues to have mild orthopnea and complains of dizziness, but has not had any chest pains. The patient reports taking her medications faithfully, though she cannot name any of them except the aspirin. Patient has history of ischemic heart disease, end-stage LV systolic dysfunction.  Patient is much improved compared to the last visit in September, but records show a recent change in medications.
PHYSICAL EXAMINATION: 
HEENT: Head normocephalic. Eyes, no evidence of anemia or jaundice. Oral hygiene is good. 
NECK: Supple. JVP is flat. Carotid upstroke is good. 
LUNGS: CTAB. 
CARDIOVASCULAR: PMI felt in fifth left intercostal space 0.5-inch lateral to midclavicular line. First and second heart sounds are normal in character. S4. There is a II/VI systolic murmur best heard at the apex.  Pulses 2+.
ABDOMEN: Soft. There is no hepatosplenomegaly.
EXTREMITIES: Patient has no appreciable edema at this time.
DIAGNOSES:
1. Atherosclerotic coronary vascular disease with old myocardial infarction.
2. Moderate to severe LV systolic dysfunction.
3. Diabetes mellitus.
4. Diabetic nephropathy and renal failure.
5. Dizziness due to hypotensive episodes
PLAN:
1. Reduce metoprolol from 25 mg daily to 12.5 mg daily.
2. Consider addition of Lisinopril.
3. Stress Echo,
4. Follow up in four months.
